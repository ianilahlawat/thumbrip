What is the correct YouTube thumbnail size?

The recommended YouTube thumbnail size is 1280 by 720 pixels, with a 16:9 aspect ratio. This is the size YouTube displays at the largest resolution across desktop, mobile, and TV, so designing at these dimensions ensures your thumbnail looks sharp everywhere. The minimum acceptable width is 640 pixels, but anything smaller than 1280 by 720 risks looking blurry on large screens. The file must be under 2MB and saved as a JPG, PNG, GIF, or BMP. Stick to 1280 by 720 and you will never have a thumbnail rejected or downscaled.

Aspect ratio matters as much as resolution. YouTube expects 16:9, the same shape as the video player. If you upload a thumbnail with a different ratio, YouTube adds black bars or crops the image, which looks unprofessional and can cut off important text or faces. Always design within a 16:9 frame from the start.

Why thumbnail size affects your views

Your thumbnail is the single biggest factor in whether someone clicks your video, and size is the foundation of a good one. A correctly sized 1280 by 720 image stays crisp when YouTube shrinks it to a tiny mobile preview or blows it up on a television. An undersized or wrong-ratio thumbnail looks soft, gets cropped awkwardly, and signals low effort — all of which lower your click-through rate. Since click-through rate feeds directly into how often YouTube recommends your video, a technically correct thumbnail isn't just about looking neat; it protects your reach.

Designing within the safe area

Even at the right size, where you place elements matters. Thumbnails are often displayed small, so keep your most important text and faces near the center and large enough to read at a glance. YouTube overlays the video's duration in the bottom-right corner, so avoid putting critical details there. Test your design by shrinking it to roughly the size of a mobile search result — if the text is still legible and the focal point is clear, it will work across every device. High contrast between your subject and the background helps the thumbnail stand out in a crowded feed.

Banner and profile picture sizes

Your channel needs more than thumbnails. The channel banner should be uploaded at 2560 by 1440 pixels, but only the central 1546 by 423 "safe area" is guaranteed to show on every device, so keep your logo and text inside that zone. The profile picture should be 800 by 800 pixels; YouTube displays it as a circle, so center your design and leave margins so nothing important is clipped. For Shorts, design vertically at 1080 by 1920 pixels to match the 9:16 full-screen format. Getting these right gives your channel a polished, consistent look.

Quick checklist for a perfect thumbnail

  • Size: 1280 × 720 pixels (16:9 aspect ratio)
  • Minimum width: 640 pixels — but always aim for 1280
  • File size: under 2MB
  • Format: JPG, PNG, GIF, or BMP
  • Keep key text and faces centered and large
  • Avoid the bottom-right corner (duration overlay)
  • Use high contrast so it stands out when small

Frequently asked questions

What size should a YouTube thumbnail be?

1280 by 720 pixels with a 16:9 aspect ratio is recommended. The minimum width is 640 pixels and the file must be under 2MB.

What is the maximum thumbnail file size?

YouTube allows custom thumbnails up to 2MB. If your file is larger, compress or resize it before uploading.

What aspect ratio do YouTube thumbnails use?

16:9, the same shape as the video player. Other ratios get cropped or have bars added, so always design in 16:9.

What file formats are accepted?

JPG, PNG, GIF, and BMP are all accepted. JPG and PNG are the most common choices for thumbnails.

What size is a YouTube banner?

Upload at 2560 by 1440 pixels, but keep important content inside the central 1546 by 423 pixel safe area so it shows on all devices.
