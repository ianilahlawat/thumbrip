How to write the perfect YouTube description

The first 2-3 lines of your description are the most important — they show up in Google search results and before the "Show more" cutoff on YouTube. Put your main keyword and a compelling hook right at the top.

YouTube's algorithm reads your description to understand your video's topic. Naturally mentioning your target keywords 2-3 times (without keyword stuffing) helps YouTube recommend your video to the right audience.

Include timestamps for longer videos — they improve watch time by helping viewers navigate to sections they care about, and Google sometimes shows timestamps directly in search results as rich snippets.
